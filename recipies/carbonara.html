Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic italian meal. The dish dates back to Roman times and was typically made from eggs, cured pork, hard cheese and black pepper.

The dish evolved into it's modern form in the early 20th century and is typically made with spaghetti pasta. This is a quick, easy dish that can easily be thrown together from leftovers on weeknights to serve the whole family.

Ingredients

  • 200g Dried Spaghetti Pasta
  • 100g Pancetta
  • 1 Block Parmesan
  • 4 Egg Yolks
  • Black Pepper
  • 4 Tbsp Olive Oil
  • 1 Clove Garlic

Steps

  1. Boil a large saucepan of water and salt generously.
  2. While the water is boiling place a large frying pan on the hob and add your Olive oil and then Garlic and Pancetta.
  3. Whisk your Egg yolks and half the parmesan together till it forms a creamy mixture. Set aside.
  4. Add Pasta to boiling water and cook till Al-Dente.
  5. Drain the pasta then toss into the pancetta and garlic.
  6. Take the frying pan off the heat and add your egg mixture.
  7. Twist the mixture into the pasta until all the pasta is thoroughly coated.
  8. Season generously with black pepper and serve.
